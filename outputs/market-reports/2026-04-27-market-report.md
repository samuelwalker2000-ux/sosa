### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** A 2025 randomised controlled trial in well-trained male runners (*ResearchGate*) confirmed that supplementing endurance training with heavy strength work (especially eccentric loading) improves both running economy and high-intensity performance in the *fatigued* state — the stage that actually determines race outcomes. The mechanism: eccentric load remodels tendon tissue and improves stiffness, meaning you lose less energy with each foot strike when it matters most. Practically: add one heavy eccentric session per week (e.g., Nordic curls, slow-tempo split squats) rather than high-rep light work, and do it on a non-key-run day to avoid performance interference.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** The 2026 Ontario Building Code update explicitly ties commercial mechanical design to ASHRAE 62.1 for the first time as a hard code requirement (previously referenced as guidance). The most operationally significant change for practicing MEP engineers: parking structures must now enforce maximum concentrations of CO ≤ 100 ppm and NO₂ ≤ 3 ppm under the updated OBC — tighter than many legacy designs. This means demand-controlled ventilation (DCV) systems tied to CO/NO₂ sensors are effectively now code-mandatory in new Ontario parking garage builds, not optional best practice. Review existing designs against the new limit if you're working on Ontario projects tendered after the code enforcement date.

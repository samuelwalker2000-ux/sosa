**Today's insight:** A 2026 randomised control trial (*ResearchGate / Sports Medicine*) confirmed that combining heavy strength training with plyometric methods produces greater improvements in running performance than either method alone — specifically improving running economy durability and high-intensity output under fatigue. The mechanism is neuromuscular: heavy loads increase motor unit recruitment efficiency and tendon stiffness, while plyometrics train rapid force production and ground contact time.

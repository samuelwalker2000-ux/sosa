### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** A 2025 randomized controlled trial (ResearchGate) confirmed that concurrent strength training improves **running economy durability** — meaning fatigued-state efficiency — and high-intensity performance in well-trained runners, not just non-fatigued running economy. The practical implication: don't skip strength work in peak training blocks. Eccentric-focused movements (Nordic curls, slow-tempo squats, single-leg RDLs) have the strongest evidence base for transferring to running performance and injury prevention. Keep loads challenging but manageable — heavy enough to stimulate adaptation, light enough to run well the next morning.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** As of January 1, 2026, Ontario's Building Code fully transitioned to the **2024 OBC**, bringing notable HVAC compliance changes. The most significant for mechanical engineers: outdoor air intakes must now be installed at least 0.3m above adjacent surfaces (OBC 6.3.2.9); ASHRAE 62.1 is explicitly referenced as the ventilation standard baseline; and evaporative cooling towers face enhanced Legionella-prevention requirements with mandatory drift eliminators and documented water treatment protocols. Industrial projects with hazardous substances now require dedicated exhaust systems by code. Worth reviewing current designs for OBC 2024 compliance, particularly for commercial buildings with rooftop AHUs or cooling towers undergoing permitting in 2026.

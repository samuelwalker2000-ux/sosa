### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** A 2024 meta-analysis in *Sports Medicine* (Springer) confirmed that combining plyometric training with heavy resistance training produces greater improvements in running economy than either method alone — without any change in VO2max — meaning the gains are neuromuscular, not metabolic. For endurance runners, 2–3 sessions per week for 8–12 weeks of combined heavy squats/deadlifts + plyometrics (box jumps, bounding) is the most evidence-backed add-on to a running programme. The practical takeaway: don't choose between strength and plyometrics — stack them in the same training block for compounding returns on running efficiency.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** ASHRAE 90.1-2022's new **Total System Performance Ratio (TSPR)** pathway (Appendix L) is the biggest HVAC compliance shift in the current code cycle — states are required to certify adoption by early 2026. Unlike prescriptive minimum component ratings, TSPR evaluates the entire HVAC system as an integrated whole, allowing engineers to trade off individual component performance for overall system efficiency. For MEP practitioners, this means energy modelling capability and whole-system commissioning are now a competitive differentiator, not optional — designs optimised under TSPR can achieve compliance while using equipment that wouldn't individually pass prescriptive minimums, opening up more flexible system design choices.

### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** A 2026 randomized controlled trial (ResearchGate) confirmed that heavy strength training significantly improves *running economy durability* — not just baseline economy, but the ability to maintain efficiency deep into long efforts when fatigued. The mechanism is neuromuscular: heavier lifts recruit and reinforce the same fast-twitch motor units that degrade late in a race, reducing the oxygen cost per stride when it matters most. Practically: 2x/week of compound heavy lifts (deadlift, squat, single-leg press) at 3–5 sets of 4–6 reps is the minimum effective dose — keep it heavy, keep it brief, and let the running volume drive aerobic adaptation separately.

### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** A 2026 systematic review and meta-analysis confirms that combining plyometric training with high-load strength work produces greater improvements in running performance — including time trial speed and running economy — than either method alone. The mechanism is neuromuscular: heavy lifting builds force production capacity while plyometrics sharpen the rate of force development, and together they train the stretch-shortening cycle more completely. Practically: add 2 plyometric sessions (box jumps, bounding, depth drops) per week alongside your 1–2 strength days, and keep running economy as your primary metric of progress.

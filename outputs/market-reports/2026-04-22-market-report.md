### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** The American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM) released landmark 2026 Resistance Training Guidelines — the first update in 17 years. Key change: for strength development, the evidence now clearly supports lifting at **80%+ of 1RM for 2–3 sets per exercise**, not the traditional 3×10 at moderate load. For runners specifically, a randomised control trial published in 2026 found that strength training improves **running economy durability** and high-intensity performance in fatigued states — meaning the strength work you do now directly translates to your last 5km of a long run, not just raw speed. Two sessions per week targeting posterior chain (hamstrings, glutes, calves) is the minimum effective dose.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** The state certification deadline for **ASHRAE 90.1-2022** passed in March 2026 — all U.S. states were required to certify adoption or demonstrate equivalent energy savings. The most significant technical change in the 90.1-2022 cycle is the new **Total System Performance Ratio (TSPR)** pathway in Appendix L, which evaluates HVAC systems as an integrated whole rather than component-by-component. This is a major shift for MEP engineers: a system with a high-efficiency chiller but poorly designed distribution can now *fail* compliance where it previously passed, forcing tighter coordination between mechanical system selection and duct/pipe design from schematic phase. The DOE estimates 8.9% energy cost savings for commercial buildings adopting 90.1-2022 (rising to 14.8% with the new renewable energy provisions).

### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** Eccentric training — the slow, controlled lengthening of a muscle under load — is one of the most powerful tools for injury prevention in runners. Research shows it remodels tendon tissue, restores load tolerance, and significantly reduces overuse injury risk (particularly for Achilles and patellar tendon issues). Practically: add Nordic hamstring curls and slow-tempo single-leg calf raises (3–4 sec lowering phase) twice per week during any running block. This one addition consistently separates runners who stay healthy from those who break down at peak mileage.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** States are required to certify adoption of ASHRAE 90.1-2022 by early 2026 — the most significant change for HVAC engineers is the new **Total System Performance Ratio (TSPR) pathway** in Appendix L. Rather than rating individual pieces of HVAC equipment against prescriptive efficiency minimums, TSPR evaluates the *entire* HVAC system's integrated performance, rewarding highly efficient system-level design (e.g., heat recovery ventilation + variable-speed drives) even where individual components may not individually meet prescriptive tables. The standard also introduced SEER2/HSPF2 metrics for air-cooled heat pumps and COPHR (Coefficient of Performance for Heat Recovery) for systems performing simultaneous heating and cooling — key metrics to verify in equipment schedules and energy models for permit submissions this year.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** As of January 1, 2025, the U.S. EPA's Technology Transitions Rule (under AIM Act) prohibits new residential and light commercial HVAC systems from using refrigerants with GWP above 700 — effectively ending R-410A in new equipment. The industry has pivoted to A2L refrigerants including **R-454B** (Opteon XL41, GWP ~466) and **R-32** (GWP 675), classified as mildly flammable (ASHRAE Standard 34, Class 2L). Practical implication for MEP engineers in 2026: all new system designs must specify A2L-compatible equipment, with updated leak detection, ventilation, and installation requirements per ASHRAE 15 and updated mechanical codes — field technicians also require A2L-specific training and certification.

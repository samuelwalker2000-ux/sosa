### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** Athletes who extended sleep duration by ~1–2 hours per night showed measurable improvements in sprint speed, reaction time, and endurance output — outperforming those who simply trained harder without addressing recovery. For runners specifically, this matters: sleep is when glycogen replenishes, muscle tissue repairs, and motor patterns consolidate. If your training load is high but performance is plateauing, address sleep before adding more volume or intensity. Aim for 8–9 hours during high-mileage weeks. *(Source: Stanford Sleep Lab research, replicated in multiple 2025–2026 endurance athlete studies)*

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** The New York State 2025 Energy Conservation Construction Code (ECCCNYS 2025) took effect December 31, 2025 — meaning all 2026 projects in NYS are now subject to stricter HVAC requirements aligned with IECC 2024 and ASHRAE 90.1-2022. Key change for mechanical engineers: heat pumps are now the default system type for new construction and major renovations in most climate zones, with HVAC design submissions required to show Manual J load calculations, Manual S equipment selection, and Manual D duct layouts. Additionally, IECC 2024 introduces a new Total System Performance Ratio (TSPR) compliance pathway via Appendix CE — evaluating the full HVAC system including fans, pumps, energy recovery, and controls rather than individual equipment ratings. Engineers should confirm their energy modelling software is updated to support TSPR pathways before submitting permit drawings.

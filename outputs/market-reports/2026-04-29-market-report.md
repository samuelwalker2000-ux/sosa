### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** A 2026 randomized controlled trial (*Sports Medicine*) confirms that **combining plyometric training with heavy lifting outperforms either method alone** for improving running performance in well-trained athletes. The mechanism is dual: heavy strength training improves running economy (less oxygen per pace), while plyometrics enhance neuromuscular stiffness and ground contact efficiency. If you're a runner only doing one or the other, you're leaving performance on the table — 2×/week concurrent sessions of jump training + compound lifts is the evidence-backed sweet spot.

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** ASHRAE has released the technical program for its **2026 Annual Conference (June 27–July 1, Austin, TX)**, with ground source heat pumps (GSHPs) taking centre stage as the lynchpin technology for building decarbonization. ASHRAE's position is that GSHPs, despite representing a small current fraction of HVAC installations, offer the highest achievable efficiency for all-electric building systems and are particularly relevant to Ontario's electrification trajectory under updated OBC Part 12 guidance. If you're pricing or designing mechanical systems for mid-rise residential or commercial projects targeting net-zero, GSHP feasibility studies are becoming a client expectation, not a premium service. Worth tracking the conference proceedings for updated design guidance on borefield sizing and hybrid GSHP configurations.

### 9. FITNESS

**Today's insight:** Eccentric training — the slow, controlled lowering phase of a lift — is one of the highest-ROI tools for runner injury prevention. Research shows it remodels tendon tissue, restores load tolerance under fatigue, and directly improves landing mechanics. Practically: add Nordic hamstring curls and slow (4-second) eccentric heel drops to your weekly routine — 2–3 sets of 8–12 reps, 2x per week is enough to produce measurable tendon stiffness gains within 6–8 weeks. *(Source: ResearchGate, 2025–2026 systematic review on strength training and running economy durability)*

### 10. MEP ENGINEERING

**Today's technical item:** The EPA's 2026 refrigerant rule changes are now in effect, representing the most significant regulatory shift for HVAC contractors since the R-22 phaseout. New rules mandate stricter leak monitoring thresholds, revised installation requirements, and restrict which refrigerants are permitted on new equipment — the focus is on accelerating the transition to low-global-warming-potential (low-GWP) alternatives such as R-32, R-454B, and R-466A. Contractors and engineers specifying new HVAC equipment in 2026 must verify compliance with the updated 40 CFR Part 82 requirements; non-compliant installs now carry higher enforcement risk. *(Source: ASHRAE 2026 Winter Conference / ACHR News)*
